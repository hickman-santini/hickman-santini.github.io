Research. My research interests include prediction markets, automated discovery, and energy policy. I bring causal inference methods to bear on problems facing the national security and international development communities. Lately, I've been working with nLine Inc. on inferring electrical grid topology from customer-level sensor readings. I am advised by Benjamin Jantzen and Shaddi Hasan.

About Me. I grew up in Silver Spring and Royal Oak, Maryland. I received my undergraduate degree in Mathematics, with a minor in Modern Languages and Linguistics, from University of Maryland - Baltimore County (UMBC). I play a lot of music, mainly Appalachian traditional string-band music, and most recently picked up the fiddle. In non-COVID times, I loved to go to the many old-time jams in and around Blacksburg. If I weren't a computer scientist, I would be writing and performing musical accompaniment for puppet shows.
